Bio

Daniel Calvert (b. 1997, London) works predominantly with oil on canvas, his work is informed by converging interests in the unconscious, the influence of digital culture, concerns of public health, an acute awareness of the biological and environmental factors that shape human well-being. Calvert’s work is both personal and political, rooted in lived experience as a queer working-class academic as well as a broader critique of the systemic neglect that disproportionately affects marginalised communities. Born in the Royal London Hospital in Whitechapel, Calvert translates the burdens imposed on those living in such a saturated metropolitan context which serves as both subject and backdrop in his work. Stylised depictions of the moment of incidence of the destruction of genetic sequencing, namely oxidative stress, appear as gestural fulmination of colour and movement with motifs of human physiology, pathogens, and electromagnetic waves. These forms serve as metaphors for the invisible but pervasive threats faced by those living in environments where exposure to harmful materials is not a choice but an inevitability.

Statement

Through my work, I employ a personal style of surrealist automatism to visualise the pressure exerted by the pervasive contemporary artificial extracorporeal influences that one must endure in my city, London, which unfortunately at this point in history I can’t help but perceive as a site of oppressive chemical, social, and psychological tension. My compositions reflect the unseen yet deeply felt forces that impact public health, from dense electromagnetic radiation to concentration of artificial food additives in foods marketed to poorer people. I wish to contribute towards shining a light on the injustice of being fated to live a shorter life with premature chronic-pain disorders, higher propensity towards substance dependency and increased likelihood of terminal illness simply because of one’s economic background; I’ve witnessed such fate in most of my immediate family members and have developed a staunch determination to break the cycle. Through my art and my sharing of the esoterica of health and nutrition I wish to inspire the plausibility of an upward trajectory for one’s health, both physical and mental, and to paint physiological vitality as a basic human right to be awarded to all.
